About

Ms. Surbhi is currently working at the Centre for Oceans, Rivers, Atmosphere and Land Sciences (CORAL), Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur as a Research scholar. Her research area is application of Remote sensing and GIS in Forestry. She has done integrated MTECH in Geoinformatics from Central university of Jharkhand (2013-2018). She has worked on “Analysis of satellite derived surface urban heat Island in Jamshedpur and Rourkela district” as her BTECH project whereas her MTECH thesis was on “Characterization of vegetation phenology over Mahanadi delta region, Odisha using time series MODIS NDVI data”.

Research focuses on:
Ms. Surbhi has focused on the following research area;
  1. Vegetation Biophysical parameter estimation of vegetation through remote sensing
  2. Crop yield estimation using Geo-spatial technique
  3. Hyperspectral remote sensing for vegetation parameter characterization
  4. Species identification, classification and mapping.
  5. Phenological metrics extraction of vegetation using time series MODIS data
  6. In-situ data collection for chlorophyll, LAI, biomass by relevant instruments (LAI-2200, SPAD, Spectroradiometer etc.)
  7. Assessment of lab based chlorophyll pigment of vegetation
  8. Measurement of urban heat island effect using satellite remote sensing data
Contribution to Project's
  • Study on Chlorophyll Concentration Estimation and Plant Diversity Assessment in Bhitarkanika and Sunderbans Mangrove Forests. (Sponsored: Space Applications Centre, Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO), Ahmedabad)
