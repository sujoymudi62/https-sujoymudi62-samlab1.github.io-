Research focuses on:

Rajendra focuses on fields of biodiversity, community ecology and climate change, which account species-environment relationships, species distribution modelling, forecasting plant invasions and climate change. His PhD research so far has produced a state¬of¬the¬art of the Himalayan ecological research where he has established that energy is a prime factor in plant richness of Western Himalaya and soil in the East Himalaya. His study reports the possibilities of plant invasions to the Himalaya along its north-eastern direction under the future climate change. Exploring the determinants of the Himalayan plant richness along its east-west dichotomy is novel and provides valuable inputs for ecology of mountain science, where the potential threats of changes in precipitation regime on plant richness of Eastern Himalaya has critical implications under the future climate change. He has experiences in entomology during his MPhil research useful to understand the economics of horticulture and crop production. His basics in Fishery science at MSc level add values to agriculture research. His special interests in biostatistics and spatial ecology and capacity to analyse big data using R and ArcGIS are big advantages to map species distribution and their invasion potential under the future climate change. His experiences in wildlife research pertaining to mammalian ecology (e.g., blackbuck, langur and bats) links biodiversity to socio-economy of stakeholders. Besides, his expertise in mountain studies along the elevation gradient has an extra edge in biodiversity research of mountain ecosystems.
